Tell Trenton: Fix the Formula. Fund Our Schools.

New Jersey’s public schools are ranked among the best in the nation, but the state's broken school funding formula is punishing communities like Audubon.

The state relies on an outdated "Local Fair Share" wealth multiplier to determine how much a town can afford to pay for its schools. This multiplier relies on abstract property values rather than the actual realities of our municipal budget. The cascading effects are devastating:

  • Unpredictable Budgeting: School administrators are forced to guess their state aid year over year, making long-term planning impossible.
  • Job Losses: When the formula shortchanges our district, the only way to balance the budget is by cutting essential educational staff and programs.
  • Taxpayer Burden: The state shifts its constitutional responsibility onto local homeowners, forcing property taxes higher just to maintain basic infrastructure.

The solution is simple: Abolish the multiplier and fully, fairly fund districts based on actual student need.
